2016-2017 League Rules

League Philosophy:

  • This is a gentleman's league that has zero tolerance for any aggressive play or behaviour that would increase the chances of injury to any player or official.

Ground Rules (Updated 2010/OCT/30):

  • no slapshots (i.e. windup above the knee results in a whistle, face-off in offending team's zone)
  • no checking or "aggressive" body contact, play the puck at all times
  • 3 minor penalties results in expulsion from the game, and a 1 game suspension (double minor = 2 penalties)
  • Misconduct: results in 10 minute penalty
    - Game Misconduct: expulsion from game, 1st offence = 1 game suspension, 2nd = 2 games + review
    - Abuse of Official: Game Misconduct, 2 game suspension, league review
  • 1 major penalty results in expulsion from game, minimum 1 game suspension, league review
  • continued undesirable behaviour (except bad goaltending) may result in expulsion from the league
  • 1st Offensive player in crease: face-off outside blueline & warning
  • Subsequent Offensive player in crease: Goaltender interference penalty
  • no red line when invoking 2-line pass rule
  • unlimited line changes at whistle when teams are at even strength (see rule 14 !!)
  • shorthanded team may only change on the fly
  • the puck drops 15sec after a stoppage in play, ready or not
  • all face-offs to be at the nearest dot
  • puck off crossbar and out of play, face-off at nearest dot to origin of shot
  • penalties result in a face-off in the defensive zone of penalized team
  • high stick on puck is immediate whistle, face-off in offending team's defensive zone (no penalty)
  • no TV timeouts after icings. (see rule 14 !!)
  • STOP TIME in the last 2 minutes of a game (if within 2 goals)
  • Time Keepers please give Pink/Yellow Game Sheets to Captains, White Game Sheet to Statistician

Tie-Breaker Rules

  1. Wins: Most total wins among the tied teams.
  2. -Head-to-Head Record: Most points in games against each other among the tied teams.
  3. -Goal Differential: Using the total goals scored during the regular season, the greater positive differential between goals for and against among the tied teams.
  4. -Most Goals For: Using the total goals scored during the regular season, the greater number of goals for among the tied teams.
  5. -Least Goals Against: Using the total goals scored during the regular season, the fewer number of goals against among the tied teams.

Playoff tiebreakers

  1. Most wins playoffs
  2. Head to head playoffs
  3. Goal differential playoffs
  4. Most goals playoffs
  5. Lowest goals against playoffs
  6. most points regular season
  7. most wins regular season
  8. goal differential regular season
  9. Coin flip.

Spares:

  • spares may only be called if fewer than 10 regular players are available.
  • maximum of 2 spares, must be same caliber as missing regular players, 10 players max.
  • spares must be registered prior to Dec 15th
  • Captains have a list of 28 or so players that have been pre identified and only those league players can spare.
