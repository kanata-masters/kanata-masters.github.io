Kanata Masters Hockey Association - News Item

New Rules Regarding The Use of Spares

Spares Policy draft Nov. 17, 2015

The Issue

The Kanata Masters League is currently well balanced, 2 points separates second and sixth place in the standings. We are however getting many negative comments and frustration that the outcome of some games is determined by elite spares added to the roster. Every team has enjoyed the supercharging of adding an elite spare as well as the sting of losing to a supercharged team.

Let's�call it a draw and fix the problem.

Spares Policy

Captains are obliged to run a tight ship and to put the onus on players to inform them well in advance that they will miss a game. (shit happens that is why we can grab spares at game time, this should be the exception not the rule)

Players the onus is on you to notify your captain if you will be missing a game, it resolves many issues if you do. Players if asked to spare at the last minute please help out the league and spare.

For health and safety reasons we encourage teams to play with a minimum of 10 skaters with 9 as your absolute minimum.If a team at game time has less than 9 players, the opposing team should loan them players as practical, or they may approve the use of an elite player. *** should they forfeit the game??Spares may come from the spares list or from team rosters, all players are rated 1 to 10, these ratings are also utilized when setting up balanced teams.

1. Roster Players are divided into 3 catagories for spares purposes

Elite players rated 9 or 10 cannot spare.

Restricted players rated 8 may spare with the following caveat, the team requiring the spare must be missing at least 1 player rated 8 or higher and if a second spare is required they must use a spare rated 7 or lower.

Regular players rated 7 or below who have no restrictions to spare.

2.Spares List Players

The league maintains a list of players who have indicated that they wish to spare in the Masters. These players are also rated and classified as either Restricted (8 and above ) or Regular (1-7)

Process to Request Spares

Request for spare must be sent to the spares coordinator prior to the day of the game, with a copy to the opposing captain.Please state which players you are missing, and if you require a defenceman or forward, an attempt to provide a suitable spare will be made.

If a spare is assigned they will play, even if a missing player unexpectedly shows up.If the player (rated 8 or higher) happens to show up the spare will be offered first to the other team.

4.Game day requests for spares

Captains will be provided a contact list for both Roster and Spare players and on game day they are responsible to find suitable spares and to notify the opposing captain. On game day including game time spares you may only use regular players (rated 1 to 7),� this puts further onus on players to keep their captains informed if they are missing a game.

Priority should be given to:

  • Players on spare list;

  • Players not playing that night;

  • Players who are playing a game that night.

Captains must notify opposing captain before game who is sparing.

5.Spare Goalies

Captains are responsible to find their own spare goalies, a list of goalies wishing to spare has been provided.Teams with low rated goalies should only ask the high ranked goalies to spare as a last resort, in keeping with the philosophy of a balanced league.As always keep the opposing captain informed.

6.Cost to spare

Players from the Spares List pay $10 game and it is a great way to introduce the players to our league and for the league to observe if they are a good fit. Most of our new players are first introduced as a spare, it is an excellent feeder system.

Note roster players and goalies do not pay to spare.
